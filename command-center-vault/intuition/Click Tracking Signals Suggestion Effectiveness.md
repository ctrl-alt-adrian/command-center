Click tracking on suggestions is the only signal for whether suggestions actually improve user searches; close the loop by measuring what users do.

Suggestion quality lives in two worlds: how well the system scores searches (internally measurable) and whether users actually act on suggestions (only measurable through behavior). RoleNext's search-intent system uses click tracking to answer the second question. When a suggestion is shown, tracking whether the user clicks it and subsequently uses that suggestion to refine their search closes the feedback loop. Without this signal, you're optimizing for quality metrics that don't correlate with product impact. This applies to any recommendation or suggestion system: measure the suggestion and measure the action.
