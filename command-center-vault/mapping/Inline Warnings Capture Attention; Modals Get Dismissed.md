Quality warnings shown inline at the point of entry (next to the input) have far higher engagement than modal dialogs.

rolenext's intent quality system displays warnings when search quality is low. Early designs showed warnings in a modal or separate section; users either didn't notice or dismissed them. Switching to inline hints (displayed below the search input, with a color palette shift) dramatically increased engagement: users noticed the warning in their primary visual focus area while typing. Contextual cues near the interaction point outperform interruptions.
