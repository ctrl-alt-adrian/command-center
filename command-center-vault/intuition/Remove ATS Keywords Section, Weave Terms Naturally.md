Competitor tools don't generate dedicated ATS keyword sections; modern systems parse full documents and humans see keyword stuffing.

The resume optimizer was generating a separate ATS Keywords section until comparison with competitor tools revealed none do this. Modern applicant tracking systems parse the entire resume document rather than scanning metadata sections. A dedicated keyword block wastes resume space (typically 1-2 lines that could highlight achievements) and signals keyword stuffing to human reviewers. Instead, weave job description terminology naturally into the summary, experience bullets, and skills sections—same coverage, better authenticity. This decision trades keyword density (marginal gain) for space efficiency and credibility (significant).
