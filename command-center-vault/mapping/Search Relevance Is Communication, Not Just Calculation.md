Ranking better results higher is half the job; users also need explicit visual signals about quality differences.

Sorting by relevance score alone doesn't tell users why one result matters more than another. RoleNext added visual signals—low-signal banners, weak-result dividers, destructive styling for irrelevant results—to surface quality differences without forcing users to interpret score magnitudes. The insight: relevance is as much about what you communicate and how you frame it as about what you calculate and rank. Design the signals into the result presentation, not just the ranking algorithm.
