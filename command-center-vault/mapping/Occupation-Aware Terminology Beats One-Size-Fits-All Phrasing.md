Adapting language to the user's target role feels more actionable than generic phrasing across all interview-prep contexts.

RoleNext adapted skill-gap language based on job context: 'mission-critical gap' for role-essential skills, 'secondary gap' for nice-to-haves. Centralizing this logic in a terminology module avoids duplicating conditional logic across the UI. Small win per instance, compound across the product—users perceive the tool as understanding their specific career path, not applying generic templates.
