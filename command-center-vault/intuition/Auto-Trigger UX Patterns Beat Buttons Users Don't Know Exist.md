Intent-quality suggestions triggered automatically on keystroke drive far higher adoption than explicit "refine" buttons buried in the UI.

rolenext's search quality system initially used an explicit "refine intent" button. Adoption was poor: users didn't know the feature existed. Switching to auto-trigger on keystroke (combined with debouncing) changed the dynamic: suggestions appeared inline while the user typed, requiring no deliberate action. Adoption and completion metrics both improved. The lesson generalizes: discoverability of features depends on whether they interrupt the primary flow or integrate into it. Integration wins.
