Generic prep optimizes for breadth; role-specific prep optimizes for career impact.

Generic interview prep tells you to study everything, optimizing for breadth. Role-specific prep tells you to study what matters for your job, optimizing for relevance. RoleNext built occupation-aware skill assessments that prioritize gaps by impact to the user's actual role, letting people focus preparation effort on skills that advance their career. This reframes interview readiness from 'how much do I know' to 'how prepared am I for this specific job?'—a shift that changes preparation strategy.
