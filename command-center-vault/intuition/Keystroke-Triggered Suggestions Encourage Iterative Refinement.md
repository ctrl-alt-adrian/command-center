Keystroke triggers feel more responsive than submit-on-blur, naturally encouraging iterative search refinement over one-shot queries.

RoleNext considered showing suggestions on keystroke vs on form blur or submit. Keystroke triggering (with debouncing) feels faster and more responsive, and it changes user behavior: the feedback loop becomes iterative rather than one-shot. Users see a suggestion, refine their input based on it, and trigger another suggestion, rather than typing, hitting submit, and waiting. The UX feels more like dialogue than transaction. This pattern works for search and query-refinement flows where rapid iteration is the goal.
