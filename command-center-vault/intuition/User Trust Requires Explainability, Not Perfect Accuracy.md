Building user trust in scoring systems often requires explainability over accuracy alone.

Interview prep tools often chase accuracy — the perfect gap prediction. But if users don't understand why they're seeing a gap, they won't trust the tool even if it's right. In RoleNext, the team chose to build rationales (impact-weighted classifications showing exactly why each gap appears) even if it meant simpler aggregation logic. A user who sees 'you need Python because 7 of 10 job postings want it' trusts that score more than a black-box accuracy number. For scoring systems in high-trust domains, explainability is often the better investment than precision.
